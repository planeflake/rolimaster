# Aetherspark's Tower

## Overview

Aetherspark's Tower is the central landmark of [[Aethergate]]: monument, civic instrument, public clock, bureaucratic fortress, and the visible shell above [[The Convergence Works]].

It is not a palace and was never meant to be one.

It is a machine the city learned to live around.

## The Cycle Beacon

The Cycle Beacon turns at the top of the tower, showing the current mana phase through color, rotation, pulse, and chime.

Citizens glance upward before opening shops, scheduling surgeries, lighting workshops, planning rites, or deciding whether to trust the day's pressure notices.

The Beacon does not predict the future. It tells the city what kind of mana the present is made of.

## Public Levels

### Clock Galleries

Public observation floors display pressure clocks, realm-balance dials, Cycle charts, and old Aetherspark instruments that still work better than newer copies.

### Aetherspark Museum

The museum tells a polished civic version of Aetherspark's life. It is accurate enough to inspire schoolchildren and incomplete enough to comfort officials.

### Civic Offices

The [[Mana Authority]], [[Ratio Office]], and [[Guild of Conduits]] maintain offices, hearing rooms, and sealed archives inside the tower.

## Restricted Levels

### The Convergence Works

The lower tower and foundations contain [[The Convergence Works]], where the city's major mana mains converge, convert, and route.

### The Old Foundations

Older chambers below the Works contain Aetherspark's early instruments, braked arrays, sealed pressure bells, and gauges that point down instead of toward [[The Nine Fonts]].

### The Quiet Shaft

The Quiet Shaft is a forbidden maintenance route descending below official maps.

Most tower workers believe it was filled in.

They are wrong.
