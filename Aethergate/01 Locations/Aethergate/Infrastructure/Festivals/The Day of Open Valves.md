# The Day of Open Valves

## Overview

The Day of Open Valves is Aethergate's controlled surplus festival.

When stored mana is close to decay and cannot be safely kept, the city spends it in public works, repairs, blessings, spectacles, clinics, experiments, and sanctioned magical revelry.

It is celebration, pressure relief, waste prevention, and civic propaganda at once.

## Civic Logic

The city would rather spend mana on public joy than pay for explosions, hauntings, converter fouling, or Dead Draw disposal.

Engineers call it scheduled pressure relief.

Children call it the best day of the year.

## Festival by Surplus

### Essence Surplus

Floating lanterns, public repairs, engine races, artificer contests, temporary lifts, animated statues, free heat, and brilliant mechanical fireworks.

### Mentalism Surplus

Dream theaters, memory plays, puzzle tournaments, shared storytelling circles, lost-item finding booths, and unnervingly accurate fortune acts.

### Channeling Surplus

Mass blessings, free funerary rites, sanctuary meals, public oath renewals, healing vigils, childbirth ward endowments, and protection rites for homes.

### Arcane Surplus

Rare and tightly controlled. If Arcane must be vented publicly, the festival becomes Violet Night: impossible fireworks, time-bells, masked patrols, prophecy booths, and curfew after midnight.

## Security

The [[Mana Authority]], [[Guild of Conduits]], city watch, and [[White Line Wardens]] all increase patrols.

Pressure readings become chaotic during the festival, making it an ideal cover for theft or sabotage.

## Saying

> "Better bright in the streets than sour in the tanks."

## Campaign Uses

- A theft happens during the festival because pressure readings are noisy.
- A batch marked Essence is actually converted Mentalism and causes shared visions across a plaza.
- A cult plans to sabotage surplus Channeling so it decays into spiritual unrest.
- The [[Ratio Office]] schedules the festival to hide a shortage.
- Old vents below [[Aetherspark's Tower]] open into something that should have remained sealed.
- A festival held during Arcane scarcity pulls patrols away from [[The Overbright Ward]].
