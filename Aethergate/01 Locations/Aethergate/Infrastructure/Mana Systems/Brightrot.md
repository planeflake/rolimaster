# Brightrot

## Overview

Brightrot is a mana overdose disease caused by long exposure to oversaturated, decayed, or badly converted mana.

It does not behave like ordinary sickness. It is partly infection, partly mana poisoning, partly unstable storage failure inside living tissue.

The largest known outbreak is contained in [[The Overbright Ward]].

## Why Arcane Matters

Brightrot spreads when excess mana becomes patterned enough to reproduce through bodies, pipes, breath, water, and contaminated objects.

Arcane disruption prevents those patterns from settling.

This makes Arcane both dangerous and necessary: the same realm feared in [[Mana Conversion]] is also what keeps Brightrot from organizing and spreading into the city.

## Stages

### Glowfever

Fever, insomnia, faint glow in veins or scars, mana sensitivity, and headaches during pressure shifts.

### Spark Cough

Breath carries tiny mana motes. Enclosed rooms develop static, sour-metallic smells, or flickering shadows.

### Glassvein

Veins, scars, teeth, nails, or old wounds crystallize. Some victims gain unstable resistance to magic or involuntary spell effects.

### Overbright

The body becomes a living mana reservoir. Healing feeds the disease unless carefully filtered.

### Bloom

The victim, object, wall, plant, or room erupts into unstable mana growths. At this stage containment matters more than cure.

## Treatment

Early Brightrot can be slowed through filtered Essence, Arcane scrambling, isolation, and careful removal of contaminated mana residue.

Late Brightrot is not reliably curable.

The best hospitals can sometimes preserve a patient until the right Cycle phase returns.

## Campaign Uses

- A patient healed with Arcane-derived Essence shows early Glowfever.
- A black-market canister carries Brightrot contamination.
- A cult tries to infect a converter to create a living reservoir.
- The city must choose between the Heartline and Overbright containment during an Arcane shortage.
