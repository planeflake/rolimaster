# Mana Absorption Arrays

## Overview

Mana absorption arrays are artificed collectors that reclaim loose, vented, leaking, or excess mana before it decays or escapes useful circulation.

In lawful civic use, they are conservation machines. In criminal use, they become theft engines.

## Purpose

Aethergate uses absorption arrays because mana cannot be wasted.

Legal arrays reclaim surgical venting, pressure overflow, factory leakage, reservoir bleed, and ambient surplus during high-pressure cycle phases.

Illegal arrays steal from conduits, valves, canisters, and protected lines.

## Aethersparkian Principle

Aetherspark did not think of mana as a substance that should be forced into obedience. He treated it as weather, music, and traffic.

His proper arrays listen before drawing, shape before storing, bleed pressure before profit, and reclaim before wasting.

## How They Work

An absorption array usually uses three linked components.

- **Sieve Rings:** nested copper, silver, or crystal hoops etched with Font-cycle marks.
- **Resonance Teeth:** clockwork combs that tune captured mana toward a stable realm behaviour.
- **Condensation Vessel:** a cooled pressure chamber where mana becomes liquid condensate or breathable vapor.

Liquid mana is easier to store, tax, and route.

Gaseous mana is fast-reacting and dangerous, making gas-capable arrays tightly regulated.

## Common Designs

### Aetherspark Sieve

The original civic design. It is large, quiet, and deliberately inefficient. The inefficiency is the safety feature: the array leaves enough mana in the air for people, wards, lamps, spirits, and lesser devices.

### Rooftop Dew Harp

A legal household, guildhall, or hospital-roof model that condenses small amounts of loose mana overnight.

### Fog Lung

A portable gas collector used by miners, airship crews, and emergency engineers. Cheap black-market versions produce dangerous mana inhalers.

### Underworks Siphon Net

A concealed illegal array built into tunnels, drains, and abandoned pipework. It leeches pressure from nearby conduits and can make theft appear to happen before mana reaches official valves.

### Font-Bloom Engine

A rare experimental array that opens during specific Cycle alignments. Aetherspark may have used one beneath the failed flying boat that became [[The Smouldering Cog]].

## Failure Signs

- Blue-white frost on copper pipes
- Drinks vibrating in shallow rings
- Breath fogging indoors during warm weather
- Hair rising toward a wall, grate, or hatch
- Clockwork devices ticking in the wrong rhythm
- A sour-metallic smell before gas ignition

## Hazards

An array that draws too aggressively creates a mana hollow where spells misfire, wards weaken, emotions flatten, and living creatures feel tired without knowing why.

The most feared failure is silent inversion, where the array stops collecting loose mana and begins drinking from living bodies, prepared spells, bound items, or nearby conduits.

## Campaign Uses

- A hospital rooftop Dew Harp is hiding an illegal siphon function.
- Bronn Gearwright identifies a pressure pattern as an array breathing.
- A legal civic array has been retuned during an Arcane surge and now drinks from the wrong current.
- Someone uses old Aetherspark safeguards to hide a newer theft.
