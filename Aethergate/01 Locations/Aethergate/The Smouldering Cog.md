# The Smouldering Cog

The Smouldering Cog is one of the most famous taverns in [[Aethergate]], built inside the restored shell of one of [[Aetherspark, Architect of the Currents|Aetherspark's]] earliest flying boat prototypes.

The vessel never flew properly. According to local legend, it rose twelve feet, spun sideways, clipped a bell tower, and landed in what is now [[Cogmarket]]. Rather than scrap it, Aetherspark supposedly laughed, bought the surrounding plot, and declared:

> "If she will not sail the skies, then let her carry stories instead."

Today the old hull forms the main tavern hall. Brass ribs curve overhead like the belly of a clockwork whale. Old propellers hang from the ceiling. The bar is built from the original boiler casing. Warm copper pipes run along the walls, carrying harmless orange mana-glow that gives the tavern its signature smouldering light.

From the street, the Cog still looks like a medium-sized airship that gave up on the sky and settled into a wiser career. Its grounded hull rests on brick pylons and old landing struts. A timber balcony runs along the upper deck, crowded with pipeworkers, airship hands, and anyone hoping to watch Cogmarket without being stepped on by it.

## Atmosphere

The Cog is noisy, warm, cramped, and alive.

It is popular with:

- Airship crews
- Pipeline workers
- Junior artificers
- Adventurers
- Mana Authority inspectors off duty
- Smugglers pretending not to be smugglers

The tavern is respectable enough that officials drink there, but rough enough that nobody asks too many questions.

## Why the Party Starts Here

The party begins in the tavern on a night when several minor mana thefts across Aethergate have become the main topic of gossip.

Possible reasons they are present:

- They were hired as cheap muscle by a pipeline foreman.
- They are waiting for a contact from the [[Mana Authority]].
- They are looking for work at the adventurers' board.
- They are investigating black-market mana inhalers.
- They are simply in the wrong tavern when trouble starts.

## Notable Features

### The Upper Deck Balcony

The old deck rail has been rebuilt into a public balcony overlooking Cogmarket.

On festival nights, lanterns hang from the rigging and the balcony fills before sunset.

### The Boiler Bar

A large circular bar built around the original mana-boiler. It no longer powers flight, but it does keep drinks warm and occasionally whistles when the city mana pressure changes.

### The Suspended Keel

The original keel hangs above the common room. People nail job notices, bounty slips, missing-person notes, and unpaid tabs to it.

### The Captain's Alcove

A private booth built from the old cockpit. It is used for quiet meetings, illegal deals, and respectable business that looks suspiciously similar.

### The Broken Propeller Stage

A small performance platform made from one of the craft's ruined propellers. Bards, street preachers, failed inventors, and drunken airship crews all perform here.

### The Engine Shrine

A small candlelit corner where artificers leave tiny gears, burnt fuses, or failed inventions in honour of Aetherspark.

## Secrets

- The tavern sits above an old maintenance shaft leading into [[The Underworks]].
- One of the decorative pipes is actually connected to a minor illegal siphon.
- The owner knows more about smuggling routes than she admits.
- A hidden compartment in the Captain's Alcove contains an early sketch by Aetherspark showing underground mana currents.
