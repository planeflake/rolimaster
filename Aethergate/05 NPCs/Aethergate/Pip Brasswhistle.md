# Pip Brasswhistle

## Overview

Pip is a young street runner who delivers messages, food, spare parts, and occasionally things he knows better than to ask about.

He is quick, nervous, and knows half the backways in [[Cogmarket]].

## Appearance

- Young halfling
- Oversized coat
- Goggles usually worn on forehead
- Carries a battered tin whistle
- Always eating something

## Personality

Pip talks fast, lies badly, and runs very well.

He wants to seem like a hardened street operator but is clearly scared by the mana thefts.

## Qualities

- Fast on rooftops and under tables
- Knows Cogmarket backways
- Wants to do the right thing before he knows what that is
- Remembers wrappers, smells, and delivery marks

## Flaws

- Lies from fear, not skill
- Touches things he should leave alone
- Thinks running counts as a plan
- Desperate to seem useful

## Rolemaster Notes

Suggested profession: Rogue or Thief.

Useful skill areas: Stalk and Hide, Tumbling, Streetwise, Pick Pockets, Perception, Running, Lie Perception.

In play, Pip is a clue carrier and a frightened witness, not the architect of the crime.

## Connection to the Starting Quest

Pip unknowingly delivered tap parts, blue-waxed packets, and faulty-looking condenser pieces to a middleman connected to the thefts.

He thought they were faulty condenser parts and festival valve fittings.

## What He Knows

- The packages were always warm or faintly frosted.
- The buyer wore a blue scarf and had a burn scar on one cheek.
- Deliveries were left near an old pressure hatch below [[The Smouldering Cog]].
- He once heard someone mention "the Deep Route."
- The blue wax on the packets matches residue near the hidden tap.

## Secret

Pip stole one small brass sensor tooth and hid it in the tavern's Engine Shrine.

He does not know it is part of the device that made the pipe report false pressure.
