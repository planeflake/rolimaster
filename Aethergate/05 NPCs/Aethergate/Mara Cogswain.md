# Mara Cogswain

## Overview

Mara Cogswain owns [[The Smouldering Cog]]. She is a sharp-eyed former airship quartermaster with a brass prosthetic hand, a dry sense of humour, and an unmatched ability to hear trouble before it reaches the door.

## Appearance

- Late middle age
- Short silver-streaked hair
- Brass prosthetic right hand
- Heavy apron with hidden pockets
- Wears an old airship officer's coat on cold nights

## Personality

Mara is practical, protective, and impossible to intimidate inside her own tavern.

She likes people who pay their tab, keep their word, and do not bleed on the good floorboards.

## Qualities

- Hears trouble before it reaches the bar
- Knows old airship and smuggling habits
- Protects staff and regulars fiercely
- Can calm a room without raising her voice

## Flaws

- Keeps useful secrets too long
- Assumes officials will misunderstand the truth
- Has old debts from her quartermaster days
- Refuses to abandon the Cog even when she should

## Rolemaster Notes

Suggested profession: Rogue, Fighter, or Mariner-veteran.

Useful skill areas: Leadership, Trading, Region Lore, Perception, Brawling, Cookery, Streetwise.

In play, Mara controls access to the tavern, the balcony, the Engine Shrine, and the old pressure hatch.

## What She Knows

- Several pipeline workers have been drinking more heavily since the thefts began.
- A young runner named [[Pip Brasswhistle]] has been carrying suspicious packages.
- Someone has been using old Underworks access beneath the tavern.
- The city mana pressure has felt "wrong" recently.
- The Cog's boiler whistle changed before the visible pressure surge.

## Secrets

Mara used to smuggle supplies during wartime. She still knows old routes through the city and can identify professional smugglers by habit alone.

She is not currently involved in the mana thefts, but some people assume she is.

## Useful Dialogue

> "In Aethergate, dear, the difference between a merchant and a criminal is usually paperwork."

> "Aetherspark built this old wreck to fly. It chose a wiser life."

> "If you're looking for trouble, check the pipeline crews. If you're looking for the truth, check who paid them."
