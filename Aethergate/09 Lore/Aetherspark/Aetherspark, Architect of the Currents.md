# Aetherspark, Architect of the Currents

## Overview

Aetherspark is remembered as the greatest artificer who ever lived.

He transformed mana from a local mystical force into the foundation of modern civilization.

Before Aetherspark, cities rose where mana naturally emerged. After Aetherspark, mana could be moved, stored, regulated, refined, and sold.

## Titles

- The First Artificer
- Architect of the Currents
- Father of the Grid
- Builder of Aethergate
- The Flame Within Iron

## Major Inventions

- Long-distance mana conduits
- Regulated mana reservoirs
- Mana pressure valves
- Early [[Mana Absorption Arrays]]
- Early flying vessels
- Clockwork navigation engines
- The first stable city-scale mana grid
- [[Aetherspark's Tower]]
- The Cycle Beacon
- The foundations of [[The Convergence Works]]

## Aetherspark's Failed Flying Boat

One of his earliest flying boat prototypes never achieved stable flight. Its shell now forms [[The Smouldering Cog]], a famous tavern in [[Aethergate]].

Some scholars consider the vessel a failure.

Artificers disagree.

They say it proved three things:

1. Mana could lift heavy machinery.
2. Clockwork stabilizers could survive mana pressure.
3. A bad landing can still become a good business.

## Hidden Truth

Late in life, Aetherspark may have discovered that the mana currents did not begin at the surface Fonts.

A fragment from one disputed journal reads:

> "The currents do not begin where we believe they do. Something drinks from them below."

Most historians consider this metaphorical.

It is not.

Some of his final array sketches do not show devices drawing mana down from the sky or outward from the Fonts. They show carefully braked instruments listening for something beneath the city that was already drinking from the currents.
