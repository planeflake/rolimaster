# Session 01 - Minor Mana Thefts

## Starting State

Cycle 1127, [[The Day of Open Valves]].

This is the first full citywide Open Valves celebration in seven years. The streets outside [[The Smouldering Cog]] are bright with legal surplus magic, old songs, pipe-steam, and people pretending not to worry about why the festival disappeared for so long.

Public truth: Aethergate has a surplus to spend before decay.

Private truth: the surplus is real but strange, and some pressure reports do not agree with the official numbers.

## Opening Scene

The party begins inside [[The Smouldering Cog]], a tavern built into the shell of one of [[Aetherspark, Architect of the Currents|Aetherspark's]] failed flying boats.

The common room is packed for Open Valves. Pipeline workers argue over pressure readings. Airship crews drink before departure. A junior artificer demonstrates a tiny clockwork bird that repeatedly crashes into soup bowls.

Outside, Aethergate's mana lamps bloom in festival colors.

Inside, the Cog's old boiler whistles in harmony with the city valves.

## Entertainer's Introduction

[[Lira Copperquill]] stands on the Broken Propeller Stage with her gear-lute tucked under one arm and raises a mug toward the balcony.

> "Friends, debtors, engineers, inspectors pretending they are off duty, and anyone who came in through the window: welcome to the Smouldering Cog on the first proper Day of Open Valves in seven years. If the pipes sing, sing back. If the floor shakes, blame Aetherspark. If your drink sparks blue, pay before you ask questions. Tonight, Aethergate remembers how to be bright."

## Inciting Incident

During the toast, the old boiler gives a second whistle that is too low, too long, and not part of the festival rhythm.

A hidden pipeline tap beneath the tavern begins pulling from a minor service line and spoofing the surface sensor so the pipe reports more mana than it actually carries.

The tap was installed to look like crude smuggling. A closer inspection shows it is doing something subtler: altering readings so the pipeline appears fuller after the theft.

Before the tap overheats, the party can notice:

- A low boiler groan under the festival whistle
- Copper pipes sweating blue-white frost
- Drinks vibrating in shallow rings
- Mara Cogswain becoming suddenly alarmed
- A pressure gauge behind the bar rising while the lamps dim
- [[Bronn Gearwright]] stopping mid-sentence to listen

## Possible Resolutions

- Shut the hidden tap before it cooks the service line.
- Cool the frost-seized fitting safely.
- Use magic to vent or stabilize the pressure.
- Trace the spoofed sensor thread before it burns out.
- Evacuate the tavern.
- Fail, causing a pressure burst, pipe steam, panic, and a temporary blackout in the Cog.

## Aftermath

[[Inspector Elara Voss]] arrives with Mana Authority officers.

She quickly realizes this was not a normal festival pressure fault.

The tap hardware is cheap, obvious, and poorly hidden.

The sensor spoof is not.

## Clues

- The tap clamp is obvious enough to frame ordinary smugglers.
- The sensor thread has been altered to report higher remaining pressure after the draw.
- The spoofed signal is too precise for street criminals.
- [[Pip Brasswhistle]] recognizes the blue wax used on a delivery packet.
- [[Bronn Gearwright]] identifies the resonance as "wrong."
- The line lost resonance before the registered surface valve.
- A scrap of blackened blue cloth is caught behind the pipe bracket.
- Blue-white frost suggests the tap briefly over-condensed ambient mana.
- Someone in the tavern quietly leaves during the confusion.

## Main Suspects

- Pip Brasswhistle, frightened runner
- Unknown buyer with blue scarf and burn scar
- Pipeline workers with gambling debts
- A smuggling crew recently arrived from Tideglass
- Someone with access to Mana Authority sensor tolerances

## Clue List

### Easy Clues

- The tap is real and was installed beneath the tavern.
- Someone wanted the incident to look like crude pipeline theft.
- Pip delivered parts wrapped in blue wax paper.
- Bronn can tell the pipe reading and the pipe sound do not agree.

### Closer Inspection

- The pressure report was falsified upward.
- The tap did not simply steal mana; it changed what the system believed was still present.
- The altered sensor would hide losses elsewhere in the same line.
- The spoof burned out when festival pressure surged.

### Deep Clues

- The line was touched before the surface valve.
- The pattern resembles an illegal [[Mana Absorption Arrays|siphon net]] breathing in pulses.
- Someone is testing how to make the city's pipeline records lie.
- The apparent smuggling is camouflage for infrastructure manipulation.

## Session Goal

By the end of the session, the party should have reason to investigate pipeline tapping, spoofed sensors, and why the city's mana records are reporting more content than the pipes actually hold.
