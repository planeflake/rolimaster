# Training Package Rules

Training packages represent extended experience, education, or employment. They may be purchased with development points during apprenticeship development. A GM may also allow packages later in a character's career when the character spends the required training time.

## Types

- **Lifestyle (L):** Broad life experience. A character normally has at most one lifestyle package.
- **Vocational (V):** Occupational training. A character may purchase multiple vocational packages if the GM allows it.

## Benefits

A package may grant:

- Skill-category ranks and skill ranks
- Extra stat-gain rolls
- Starting-money adjustments
- Rolls for equipment, contacts, or other special benefits

## Later Training

When a package is acquired after apprenticeship:

- The character spends the listed continuous training time.
- Major interruptions pause the training.
- The character pays the package's profession-specific development-point cost.
- Starting-money adjustments do not apply.
- Lifestyle changes should remain exceptional and normally be limited to one per level.
- A training package cannot raise a skill above rank 10.

## Special Benefits

Special benefits are listed with target numbers. The GM rolls for entries in order. A result over 100 grants the benefit. After a benefit is gained, later chances are reduced as described in the source rules. If no benefit is gained, the last listed benefit is normally granted.
