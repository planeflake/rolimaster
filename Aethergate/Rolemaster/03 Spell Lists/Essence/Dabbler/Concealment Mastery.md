# Concealment Mastery

## Summary

Dabbler spells for hiding the caster or objects through shadows, illusions, displacement, darkness, and invisibility.

## Realm

- [[Essence]]

## Profession / Category

- [[Dabbler]]

## Spell Grid

| Level | Spell | Area of Effect | Duration | Range | Type | Description |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Shadow | caster | `10 min/lvl` | self | U | The caster and carried objects appear shadowed, making them almost invisible in darkness and often granting a substantial bonus to Stalking/Hiding maneuvers. |
| 2 | Unseen III | 3 targets | `24 hours` | `10'/lvl` | E | Makes up to three objects invisible until the duration ends, the object is struck violently, or it makes a violent move such as an attack. |
| 3 | Conceal I | 1 object | `4 hrs/lvl` | touch | E | Gives one inanimate object the visual appearance of a similar object, within normal size limits; this is an illusion. |
| 4 | Locate Object | 1 object | - | `5'/lvl` | I | Finds an inanimate object if the caster can uniquely describe it. |
| 5 | Darkness | `2'R/lvl` | `10 min/lvl` | `10'` | E | Creates darkness around the touched point, moving with the touched object or being if applicable. |
| 6 | Secret I | 1 object | `4 hrs/lvl` | touch | U | Gives one inanimate object both the visual and tactile appearance of another object, within normal size limits; this is an illusion. |
| 7 | Unseen V | 5 targets | `24 hours` | `10'/lvl` | E | As Unseen III, but affects up to five objects. |
| 8 | Displacement I | caster | `1 min/lvl` | self | E | Offsets the caster's apparent position; attacks against the caster have an initial `10%` automatic miss chance that drops by `5%` after each miss. |
| 9 | Conceal III | 3 objects | `4 hrs/lvl` | touch | E | As Conceal I, but affects up to three objects. |
| 10 | Unseen X | 10 targets | `24 hours` | `10'/lvl` | E | As Unseen III, but affects up to ten objects. |

## Special Notes

- Locate Object requires an unambiguous description, including approximate dimensions and at least two other identifying features.
- Darkness references the source's general darkness rules.
- Conceal and Secret effects are illusions and can be detected as such.
