# Thieving Law

## Summary

Dabbler spells for assessing wealth, marking and locating targets, finding buyers, valuing objects, and identifying magical properties.

## Realm

- [[Essence]]

## Profession / Category

- [[Dabbler]]

## Spell Grid

| Level | Spell | Area of Effect | Duration | Range | Type | Description |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Gauge Wealth | 1 target | - | `10'` | P | Gives the caster a sense of the target's total wealth or net worth, which may include non-coin assets. |
| 2 | Weigh Pockets | 1 target | - | `10'` | P | Tells the caster approximately how much wealth the target carries on their person. |
| 3 | Mark Target | 1 target | `1 hr/lvl` | touch | P | Magically marks one target for later use with Locate Target; only one target can be marked at a time. |
| 4 | Find Buyer I | `500'R/lvl` | - | self | I | Locates the nearest person in the area who wants a specific object; the caster must touch the object while casting. |
| 5 | Locate Target | `50'R/lvl` | - | self | I | Reveals the exact direction and distance to a marked target. |
| 6 | Jewel/Metal Assessment | 1 object | - | self | I | Assesses the value of jewels and metals within `10%`, including different cultural values known to the caster; it does not assess craftsmanship or magic. |
| 7 | Item Assessment | 1 object | - | self | I | As Jewel/Metal Assessment, but also accounts for the item's craftsmanship; magical properties are not evaluated. |
| 8 | Find Buyer II | `500'R/lvl` | - | self | I | As Find Buyer I, but locates someone who both wants the object and is willing to acquire it now. |
| 9 | Detect Power | 1 object | - | self | I | Detects whether an item has magical power, but not the realm or amount of power. |
| 10 | Item Analysis I | 1 object | - | self | I | Gives the caster a `10%` chance to identify an enchanted object's abilities, rolled separately for each ability; the same item cannot be checked again until the caster gains a level. |

## Special Notes

- The GM decides what ownership and wealth mean in the setting.
- The source points to its general information-spell rules for additional handling.
