# Highland Culture

## Summary

Highland cultures occupy rugged hills, uplands, broken valleys, and hard-to-cross country. They are often clan-based, independent, and accustomed to raids, feuds, herding, scouting, and rough travel.

## Environment

- Highlands, moors, steep valleys, rocky hills, upland farms, and fortified clan settlements.
- Communities are separated by terrain and weather, creating strong local identities.
- Paths, passes, grazing rights, and defensible ground matter politically.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Durable wool, leather, cloaks, boots, wraps, and weatherproof layers are common.
- Decoration may include clan colors, brooches, woven patterns, weapons, charms, and trophies.
- Gear must survive rain, mud, rock, and long walks.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Highlanders often value kinship, toughness, honor, local memory, and independence.
- Outsiders may be judged by courage, oath-keeping, and respect for clan boundaries.
- Common fears and taboos may involve darkness, earth spirits, haunted hills, ancient cairns, and broken oaths.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The culture suits scouts, warriors, rangers, bards, herders, guides, hunters, and clan champions.
- Common skill themes include climbing, survival, weather watching, singing, storytelling, regional lore, weapon training, animal handling, and scouting.

## Background Languages

- The exact language choices should be transcribed with the rank table pass.
- For Mana World, highland cultures should use hill tongues, clan dialects, nearby trade speech, and older languages tied to upland ruins.

## Outfitting Options

- Light armor, shields, spears, axes, swords, bows, cloaks, travel food, rope, and hill-country camping gear.
- Heavy armor may appear among chiefs, champions, and militarized groups, but it is often awkward in steep country.

## Subcultures

- Highland Barbarian.
- Highland Militaristic.

## Character Hooks

- Clan hostage, pass warden, feud survivor, hill bard, cattle raider, upland scout, oathbound champion, or keeper of an old cairn secret.

## Mana World Notes

Highland cultures can guard hilltop font-sites, standing stones, and wind-worn paths where mana changes aspect with weather and elevation.
