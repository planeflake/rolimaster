# Rural Culture

## Summary

Rural cultures grow from farms, villages, orchards, mills, pastures, and small market communities. They are shaped by food, seasons, family, animals, land rights, neighbors, and the steady labor of ordinary survival.

## Environment

- Villages, farms, ranches, orchards, hamlets, mills, fields, pastureland, and small rural shrines.
- Communities are usually local, interdependent, and strongly aware of harvest, weather, and inheritance.
- Even peaceful villages may produce tough, practical characters.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Simple work clothes, boots, aprons, cloaks, caps, wool, linen, leather, and patched garments are common.
- Decoration is modest: festival ribbons, family tokens, carved wood, embroidery, simple jewelry, and religious charms.
- Clothing is built for labor, weather, and repair.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Rural folk often value reliability, thrift, patience, hospitality, local reputation, and practical knowledge.
- They may distrust city luxury, foreign manners, or people who do not understand land and animals.
- Common fears include famine, disease, failed crops, raiders, tax collectors, and losing the family holding.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The culture suits farmers, healers, hunters, militia, priests, craft workers, animal handlers, scouts, and reluctant adventurers.
- Common skill themes include agriculture, animal handling, first aid, foraging, local lore, survival, weather watching, craft work, trading, and simple weapons.

## Background Languages

- The exact language choices should be transcribed with the rank table pass.
- For Mana World, rural cultures should use local speech, market speech, temple language, and neighboring regional dialects.

## Outfitting Options

- Work clothes, tools, knives, staves, bows, leather armor, packs, preserved food, family tokens, and small livestock or their value.
- The source includes livestock as starting-value examples, including cows, sheep, goats, and geese.

## Subcultures

- Rural Militaristic.
- Rural Nomadic.

## Character Hooks

- Village healer, farmer's child, runaway apprentice, militia veteran, tax fugitive, miller's heir, shrine keeper, or witness to a field touched by wild mana.

## Mana World Notes

Rural cultures anchor Mana World to ordinary life: harvests, local shrines, font-blessed wells, road chapels, and the pressure of larger powers on small communities.
