# Sylvan Culture

## Summary

Sylvan cultures live in forests, deep woods, sacred groves, and tree-shadowed settlements. They are shaped by plants, animals, stealth, seasonal abundance, forest law, and the duty to preserve a living environment.

## Environment

- Ancient forests, woodland villages, tree dwellings, hidden camps, groves, hunting trails, and forest shrines.
- Communities often blend shelter, ritual, food, and defense into the natural landscape.
- Travel follows animal paths, rivers, canopy routes, and remembered clearings.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Leather, soft cloth, bark fiber, fur, cloaks, greens, browns, and quiet practical garments are common.
- Decoration may include leaves, feathers, carved wood, beads, embroidery, animal motifs, and forest charms.
- Armor is usually light and quiet unless a militarized subculture changes the norm.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Sylvan folk often value patience, beauty, memory, balance, secrecy, and defense of the forest.
- They may distrust axes, cities, smoke, careless hunters, or rulers who see woods only as resources.
- Common fears include exile from the forest, fire, blight, logging, corruption of sacred groves, and the loss of old songs.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The culture suits rangers, scouts, animists, healers, hunters, bards, herbalists, wardens, and forest mystics.
- Common skill themes include acrobatics, ambush, light armor, body development, climbing, craft work, dancing, fauna lore, first aid, foraging, flora lore, hiding, observation, stalking, survival, tracking, and bows or spears.

## Background Languages

- The exact language choices should be transcribed with the rank table pass.
- For Mana World, sylvan cultures should include wood-speech, local forest dialects, elven tongues where appropriate, and languages used by spirits or old grove guardians.

## Outfitting Options

- Leather armor, cloaks, bows, spears, knives, herbal kits, snares, climbing gear, travel food, musical instruments, and tokens from a grove or clan.
- Heavy armor is rare except for unusual champions or outside-trained characters.

## Subcultures

- Sylvan Barbarian.
- Sylvan Nomadic.
- Woodfolk.

## Character Hooks

- Grove warden, forest singer, hunted scout, healer of a blighted wood, nomadic bowyer, tree-village exile, or messenger from a hidden green court.

## Mana World Notes

Sylvan cultures connect easily to living mana: root networks, old groves, font-fed springs, speaking trees, and places where the [[Nine Fonts]] breathe through seasonal change.
