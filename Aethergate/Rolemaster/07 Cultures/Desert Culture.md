# Desert Culture

## Summary

Desert cultures are defined by arid lands, scarce water, long travel, harsh heat, cold nights, and social systems that protect survival knowledge. They may be nomads, oasis dwellers, raiders, merchants, soldiers, or urban peoples whose cities depend on desert routes.

## Environment

- Dunes, salt flats, rocky wastes, dry highlands, badlands, wadis, caravan roads, oases, and desert cities.
- Water, shade, animals, and trust are strategic resources.
- Travel and settlement patterns follow wells, seasonal grazing, trade routes, and defensible refuges.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Loose layered clothing, veils, robes, wraps, sandals or boots, and durable cloth are common.
- Leather, wool, cotton, bright dyes, metal ornaments, and clan or caravan marks appear where wealth allows.
- Clothing protects against sun, sand, heat, and cold nights.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Desert folk often prize endurance, hospitality under law, memory, loyalty, and hard bargaining.
- Wastefulness, water theft, and soft living may be treated with contempt.
- Religion often centers on lawgivers, sun or storm powers, guardian ancestors, oases, and the moral weight of hospitality.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The culture supports warriors, traders, scouts, guides, priests, animal handlers, thieves, and caravan guards.
- Common skill themes include riding, survival, weather watching, foraging, trading, weapon use, regional lore, endurance, animal handling, and desert navigation.

## Background Languages

- The exact language choices should be transcribed with the rank table pass.
- For Mana World, desert cultures should include caravan trade tongues, oasis dialects, and neighboring regional languages.

## Outfitting Options

- Water skins, desert clothing, tents, riding animals, trade goods, knives, spears, bows, shields, and practical light armor.
- Heavy armor is difficult in the desert, though wealthy or militarized groups may use mail or specialized armor for battle.

## Subcultures

- Desert Brigand.
- Desert Militaristic.
- Desert Urban.
- Desert Urban Upper Class.
- Desert Urban Middle Class.
- Desert Urban Underclass.

## Character Hooks

- Caravan guide, oasis judge, sand prophet, raider defector, well-keeper, desert scout, merchant heir, or city-born underclass survivor.

## Mana World Notes

Desert cultures can read mana through heat shimmer, buried water, glassed ruins, dry lightning, and old cycle roads between font-touched oases.
