# Subterranean Culture

## Summary

Subterranean cultures live below the surface in caves, tunnels, deep cities, vaults, and underworld passages. They are formed by darkness, enclosed space, stone, hidden routes, scarce surface contact, and whatever old things share the depths.

## Environment

- Caves, tunnel networks, vault-cities, deep mines, underground rivers, ruins, and natural caverns.
- Communities rely on memory, maps, light discipline, sound, smell, hidden food sources, and defensible passages.
- The surface may be a trade partner, a myth, a threat, or a hated exile-place.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Hides, furs, leather, work garments, cloaks, masks, helmets, and protective layers are common.
- Decoration may include metal, fungus dyes, bone, stone, clan marks, carved tools, and tokens from deep places.
- Cloth and wool may be rarer depending on trade access.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Subterranean folk often value caution, memory, secrecy, endurance, and knowledge of hidden paths.
- They may be suspicious of bright light, wide open spaces, weather, and surface politics.
- Common fears include collapse, flooding, fire, monsters, surface invasion, cursed depths, and getting lost.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The culture suits delvers, scouts, miners, guards, ambushers, priests, engineers, lore keepers, and traders.
- Common skill themes include alertness, ambush, armor, caving, direction sense, history, leadership, magic ritual, metal craft, stone craft, subterranean survival, and weapon skills except missile artillery.

## Background Languages

- Black-speech, Common-speech, and Orcish are listed.
- Shadow World options include Dyari, Lugroki, and Murlogi languages.

## Outfitting Options

- Lamps, helmets, climbing tools, rope, chalk, maps, picks, knives, short weapons, shields, leather or mail armor, food suited to the depths, and compact trade goods.
- Bright outdoor gear is less important than tools that work in darkness and narrow passages.

## Subcultures

- Subterranean Deeps.
- Subterranean Militaristic.
- Subterranean Urban.
- Subterranean Urban Upper Class.
- Subterranean Urban Middle Class.
- Subterranean Urban Underclass.

## Character Hooks

- Tunnel scout, undercity guide, deep shrine initiate, escaped underclass worker, cave militia veteran, ruin mapper, or exile who saw the sky and survived.

## Mana World Notes

Subterranean cultures fit buried font-chambers, old Aethergate underworks, crystal veins, sealed cycle vaults, and communities that understand mana as pressure, resonance, and darkness.
