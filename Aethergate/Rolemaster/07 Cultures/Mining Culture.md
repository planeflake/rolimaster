# Mining Culture

## Summary

Mining cultures are built around quarrying, tunneling, ore, stone, workshops, and the settlements that grow around underground labor. They are practical, disciplined, and often strongly tied to craft, family, guild, or clan.

## Environment

- Mines, quarries, smelter towns, mountain tunnels, underground work camps, and fortified mining settlements.
- Daily life is shaped by darkness, dust, danger, tools, shifts, guild hierarchies, and control of valuable resources.
- The culture may be surface-based, subterranean, or a hybrid of both.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Heavy work clothes, leather aprons, gloves, boots, helmets, masks, and tool belts are common.
- Decoration often uses metal, gems, carved stone, guild marks, family tools, and craft tokens.
- Clothing and gear show trade, rank, wealth, and hard use.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Mining folk often value reliability, craft competence, practical courage, debt-keeping, and respect for dangerous work.
- Suspicion of outsiders may grow where mines hold secrets, wealth, or old buried hazards.
- Common fears include collapses, poisoned air, fire, monsters below, dragons, and greedy rulers.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The culture suits miners, smiths, engineers, scouts, guards, merchants, delvers, healers, and guild officials.
- Common skill themes include appraisal, armor, caving, crafts, mining, metal lore, stone lore, underground survival, direction sense, trading, and weapon training.

## Background Languages

- The exact language choices should be transcribed with the rank table pass.
- For Mana World, mining cultures should include trade speech, hill or dwarf tongues, guild cants, and any local underworld languages.

## Outfitting Options

- Work gear, helmets, lamps, tools, ropes, wedges, pack animals, leather or mail armor, practical weapons, ore samples, and craft goods.
- Wealthier characters may begin with quality tools, guild credentials, or a claim token.

## Subcultures

- Mining Militaristic.

## Character Hooks

- Guild surveyor, escaped debt miner, ore-priest, mine guard, gem appraiser, tunnel scout, claim heir, or witness to something awakened below.

## Mana World Notes

Mining cultures are a natural bridge between Rolemaster mechanics and Mana World font-lore: veins, crystals, old machines, and buried ruins can all conduct or distort mana.
