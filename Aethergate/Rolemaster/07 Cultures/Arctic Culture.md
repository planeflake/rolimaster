# Arctic Culture

## Summary

Arctic cultures are formed by snow, ice, tundra, long winters, and scarce resources. Their lives revolve around hunting, fishing, herding, shelter craft, careful travel, and the discipline needed to survive where mistakes are expensive.

## Environment

- Frozen coasts, tundra, glaciers, ice fields, taiga edges, and winterbound settlements.
- Communities are often small, mobile, kin-based, and alert to seasonal routes.
- Survival depends on knowledge of weather, animals, snow, ice, and preserved food.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Fur, hide, layered leather, heavy boots, mitts, hoods, and weatherproof wrappings are common.
- Bone, ivory, carved antler, beads, clan marks, and trophies from important hunts are common decorations.
- Clothing is built first for warmth and survival, with ornament layered into practical gear.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Arctic folk tend to respect competence, generosity within the group, restraint, and the ability to endure hardship.
- Outsiders may be judged by whether they waste food, ignore weather, or fail to understand the seriousness of travel.
- Common fears include starvation, exposure, predators, failed migrations, and supernatural winter forces.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The culture supports hunters, scouts, animists, healers, fishers, herders, guides, and weather-wise wanderers.
- Source skill themes include cold-environment survival, hunting, fishing, animal handling, first aid, tracking, regional lore, weather watching, and practical weapon use.

## Background Languages

- The exact language choices should be transcribed with the rank table pass.
- For Mana World, arctic dialects should connect to northern trade speech, clan tongues, and any local spirit or giant languages used near icebound borders.

## Outfitting Options

- Heavy winter clothing, furs, tents, sleds, snowshoes, fishing tools, hunting weapons, preserved food, and trade goods such as ivory or hides.
- Metal armor is possible but often impractical in deep cold unless carefully managed.

## Subcultures

- Arctic Militaristic.
- Arctic Nomadic.

## Character Hooks

- Ice guide, seal hunter, frost shrine keeper, caravan pathfinder, reindeer herder, exile from a winter clan, or envoy from a glacier settlement.

## Mana World Notes

Arctic cultures fit regions where mana is slow, crystalline, or seasonally locked. They can be excellent guardians of frozen font-sites or old cycle ruins sealed under ice.
