# Aerial Culture

## Summary

Aerial cultures are shaped by high mountains, cliffs, aeries, and the open sky. Their communities tend to be remote, practical, and physically capable, with customs built around climbing, weather, altitude, and the danger of exposed terrain.

## Environment

- High mountain settlements, cliff dwellings, sky-facing shrines, and isolated passes.
- Daily life favors endurance, balance, rope work, weather sense, and a readiness to move through difficult vertical terrain.
- Communities often depend on herding, hunting, guiding, scouting, and trade routes through dangerous highlands.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Clothing is simple and protective: wool, fur, leather, cloaks, gloves, boots, and tightly fitted layers.
- Decoration is restrained, often limited to practical metalwork, charms, clan tokens, feathers, or highland craft pieces.
- Outfits should make sense for wind, cold, climbing, and sudden storms.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Aerial folk often prize independence, discipline, endurance, and a clear sense of place.
- Outsiders may read them as aloof or severe, while their own communities see this reserve as practical caution.
- Their great fears are displacement, loss of the high places, and forces that make the mountains unlivable.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The source does not impose strict preferred or prohibited professions for the base culture.
- Common hobbies and adolescent skills include acrobatics, light armor, body development, camouflage, climbing, distance running, fauna lore, foraging, observation, rappelling, rope mastery, scaling, singing, mountainous survival, tightrope-walking, weather watching, and most weapon skills except missile artillery.

## Background Languages

- Common-speech, High-speech, and Hill-speech are the main listed language families.
- Shadow World options include Giant and Titan tongues.

## Outfitting Options

- Light armor, climbing gear, ropes, warm clothing, practical weapons, weatherproof packs, and compact trail supplies.
- Heavy armor is usually a poor fit for altitude, climbing, and narrow ledges.

## Subcultures

- Aerial Barbarian.

## Character Hooks

- Mountain guide, eagle-signal watcher, cliff shrine novice, storm-reader, pass warden, highland raider, or exile from an isolated aerie.

## Mana World Notes

Aerial cultures fit high aeries, mountain monasteries, sky shrines, and communities that read the [[Nine Fonts]] through wind, pressure, altitude, and storm patterns.
