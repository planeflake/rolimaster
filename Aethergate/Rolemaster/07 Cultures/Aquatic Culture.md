# Aquatic Culture

## Summary

Aquatic cultures live in, on, or around major bodies of water. They may be fully underwater societies, island peoples, lake dwellers, river communities, or coastal groups whose identity is inseparable from currents, tides, fishing, and navigation.

## Environment

- Oceans, deep lakes, reefs, shelves, river mouths, wetlands, and undersea settlements.
- Communities may be nomadic, militarized, urban, or organized around fishing, diving, trade, and aquatic travel.
- Underwater members of this culture treat three-dimensional movement and water pressure as ordinary facts of life.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Clothing is usually minimal or made from water-tolerant fibers, shells, leather, nets, scaled materials, or woven aquatic plants.
- Jewelry and decoration often use shell, bone, pearl, coral, polished stone, or traded metal.
- Armor must be compatible with swimming and underwater movement.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Aquatic cultures often value adaptability, patience, memory, seamanship, and respect for dangerous waters.
- Religion and symbolism commonly center on water, tides, storms, sea creatures, whirlpools, or deep ancestral places.
- Landbound customs can seem slow, dry, or confining to people raised in this culture.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The base culture does not strongly restrict professions.
- Common hobbies and adolescent skills include aquatic animal handling, animal mastery, animal training, body development, diving, first aid, aquatic fauna lore, aquatic flora lore, foraging, history, regional lore, riding aquatic creatures, surfing, aquatic survival, swimming, and polearm training.

## Background Languages

- Common-speech, North-speech, South-speech, and Sea-speech are listed.
- Shadow World options include Mermen and Shuluri languages, plus suitable adjacent regional or racial tongues.

## Outfitting Options

- Swimming gear, nets, knives, spears, polearms, light waterproof packs, diving tools, fishing equipment, and trade goods.
- Heavy armor is rare unless magical, ceremonial, or specifically adapted for underwater use.

## Subcultures

- Aquatic Nomadic.
- Aquatic Militaristic.
- Aquatic Urban.
- Aquatic Urban Underclass.

## Character Hooks

- Pearl diver, reef scout, tide-priest, harbor envoy, ship hunter, river messenger, undersea exile, or survivor of a drowned city.

## Mana World Notes

Aquatic cultures can tie directly to mana currents moving through rivers, sea gates, storm tides, and drowned ruins connected to the [[Nine Fonts]].
