# Littoral Culture

## Summary

Littoral cultures live along coasts, islands, river deltas, harbors, and sea roads. They are shaped by fishing, shipping, boats, storms, trade, tides, and the mix of strangers that comes with ports.

## Environment

- Seacoasts, islands, reefs, river mouths, harbors, fishing villages, and maritime cities.
- Communities range from small boat villages to busy port districts.
- Travel, trade, piracy, fishing, and navigation are everyday parts of life.

## Clothing and Decoration

- Loose shirts, tunics, trousers, wraps, boots or sandals, sailor's gear, and weatherproof cloaks are common.
- Shell, bone, glass beads, tattoos, ropework, polished metal, and foreign trade goods appear as decoration.
- Clothing is practical for boats, docks, storms, and wet work.

## Attitudes and Dispositions

- Littoral folk often value adaptability, nerve, trade sense, storytelling, and the ability to read weather and water.
- They may be unusually cosmopolitan where ports bring many peoples together.
- Fear often centers on storms, wrecks, sea monsters, raiders, plague ships, and the loss of boats or fishing grounds.

## Professions and Hobbies

- The culture supports sailors, traders, fishers, pirates, navigators, scouts, dock workers, smugglers, priests, and marines.
- Common skill themes include boating, sailing, swimming, navigation, trading, weather watching, fishing, regional lore, rope work, and weapon training.

## Background Languages

- Common-speech, Grey-elvish, North-speech, South-speech, and Sea-speech are listed.
- Shadow World options include Mermen, Shuluri, Undersea tongues, and a suitable mortal racial language.

## Outfitting Options

- Sailor's clothing, rope, knives, nets, hooks, boat tools, light armor, shields, spears, bows, trade goods, and waterproof containers.
- Heavy armor is uncommon on boats unless the character expects boarding actions or shore fighting.

## Subcultures

- Littoral Brigand or Pirate.
- Littoral Urban.
- Littoral Urban Upper Class.
- Littoral Urban Middle Class.
- Littoral Urban Underclass.

## Character Hooks

- Harbor pilot, wreck survivor, pirate deserter, tide-priest, customs runner, island envoy, dockside informant, or navigator chasing a forbidden chart.

## Mana World Notes

Littoral cultures fit Aethergate's docks, canal edges, sea gates, and trade quarters where mana travels with fog, tide, and cargo.
