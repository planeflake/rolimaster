# Dwarves

## Summary

Dwarves are rugged, practical, clannish, stubborn, and famed as miners, smiths, and artifact makers. The Shadow World equivalent is the Nomari, who share the same broad identity but have distinct setting-specific religious and physiological notes.

## Variants

- **RMFRP Dwarves:** Classic mountain and cavern folk, strongly tied to mining, craft, clan loyalty, and inherited grudges.
- **Nomari:** Shadow World Dwarves, with major settlements such as Burdok-Rhu and Baku-jagum in the source setting.

## Physical Notes

Dwarves are short, stocky, and powerfully built. They are long-lived by human standards, with exceptional stamina and strong resistance to harsh physical conditions. The source notes that Shadow World Nomari differ in their response to extreme cold.

## Mechanical Notes

- Strong endurance and physical toughness.
- Heat and cold resistance for standard Dwarves.
- Shadow World Nomari have altered cold notes and a hibernation-related exception in the source.
- Strong fit for Fighter professions.
- Less natural fit for agile, socially focused, Essence, or Mentalism paths.
- Everyman skills include craft, mining, caving, and underground survival themes.

## Languages

Extra Languages: High-speech (S5/W5), South-speech (S4/W4), North-speech (S5/W5).

Nomari extra languages: Loari (S8/W6), Dyari (S8/W6), Murlogi (S6/W6).

## Culture Notes

Dwarves guard their own language closely and tend to distrust old enemies. The source emphasizes hostility toward Orcs, Dragons, dark underground races, and, for Nomari, demons and Murlogi. Dwarven religion centers on ancestors, bloodline continuity, creator figures, and, in Shadow World, local patron deities.

## Typical Cultures

- [[Mining Culture]] is the primary fit.
- [[Subterranean Culture]] fits fully underground communities.
- [[Rural Culture]] can fit valley or support settlements.
- Urban subcultures can fit Dwarves living as smiths, merchants, or specialists in major cities.

## Character Hooks

- A Dwarf leaves a threatened mine to find the weapon, ally, or lore needed to face a Dragon, Orc band, or subterranean enemy.
- A failed city smith turns to dangerous work after losing a business or clan standing.
- A Nomari exile investigates a demonic trace in a mana vein.

## Mana World Notes

Dwarves fit naturally into Mana World as keepers of deep mana strata and ancient craft traditions. Their clan spirits could be interpreted as ancestral resonance carried through the Nine Fonts, with each bloodline remembering a different phase of the Font cycle.
