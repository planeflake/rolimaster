# Gnomes

## Summary

Gnomes are long-lived, solitary, lore-loving mortals who resemble small, wizened humans. They are hospitable and curious, but reluctant to leave their homes, treasures, books, and carefully guarded lairs.

## Variants

None listed in this source.

## Physical Notes

Gnomes are small, pale, bald or white-haired, and exceptionally long-lived for mortals. They have dark vision in night or low-light conditions but not in absolute darkness.

## Mechanical Notes

- Poor natural fit for Fighters due to low Strength.
- Broad modest bonuses make them capable in several knowledge or spell-adjacent roles.
- The source highlights Clerics, Animists, Paladins, and Magents as possible fits.
- Gnomes are skilled linguists and may take rare books as wealth.

## Languages

Starting Languages: Gnomish (S8/W8), optional Common-speech (S6/W6).

Allowed Adolescent Development: Gnomish (S10/W10), Common-speech (S8/W8), High-Elvish (S6/W6), High-speech (S8/W8).

## Culture Notes

Gnomes prefer solitary meditation over organized worship. They judge visitors by action rather than ancestry, and can be generous hosts, but they are deeply reluctant to abandon their own guarded places.

## Typical Cultures

Likely fits include [[Mining Culture]], [[Rural Culture]], [[Subterranean Culture]], or an urban scholarly subculture, depending on where the Gnome's lair or library is placed.

## Character Hooks

- A party must persuade a Gnome specialist to leave home long enough to solve a specific problem.
- A Gnome scholar travels because a text references a lost Font grammar.
- A Gnome host becomes entangled in Aethergate politics after sharing rare lore with the wrong visitor.
