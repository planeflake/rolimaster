# Fey

## Summary

Fey are immortal, often reclusive peoples bound to wild or hidden places. This consolidated source entry includes small underground tricksters, woodland revelers, miniature riders, and Shadow World forest immortals.

## Variants

- **Dwelfs:** Small, stocky Fey resembling miniature Dwarves. They live in banks, hills, and mine-adjacent underground spaces.
- **Satyrs / Fauns:** Horned, music-loving woodland Fey known for wild dancing, panpipes, and mischief.
- **Tylweth Teg / Peries:** Tiny Elven-looking rural Fey who live in caverns and grottos beneath mossy hills.
- **Lennai:** Shadow World forest immortals, rare and secretive, sometimes called the Forest People.

## Physical Notes

Fey vary widely in build, from stocky Dwelfs to slender Lennai. Several Fey peoples are small enough to suffer reduced stamina compared with larger races. Satyrs are closer to human height, while Tylweth Teg are much smaller.

## Mechanical Notes

- Fey are immortal but can die through violence or mischance.
- Dwelfs, Satyrs, and Tylweth Teg lean toward stealth, trickery, music, and agility-based roles.
- Tylweth Teg have a strong fear of iron and avoid iron or steel equipment.
- Lennai have major mystical affinities, forest ties, and vulnerability around iron.
- Several Fey variants receive discounted access to luck-related or lesser mystical/mental talents in the source.

## Culture Notes

Fey tend toward seclusion rather than conquest. Satyrs are more openly friendly but still defend their sylvan homes. Lennai are governed by Dryad-like leaders who combine temporal and sacred roles.

## Typical Cultures

- Dwelfs: hidden rural, mining-adjacent, or sylvan edge communities.
- Satyrs: [[Sylvan Culture]].
- Tylweth Teg: [[Sylvan Culture]].
- Lennai: [[Sylvan Culture]].

## Character Hooks

- A Tylweth Teg helps mortals because the problem seems delightful or interesting.
- A Lennai scout leaves the forest because iron-bearing intruders threaten a sacred grove.
- A Satyr musician hears a tune from a damaged Font and cannot resist following it.
