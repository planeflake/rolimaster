# Garks

## Summary

Garks are short, simian humanoids with prehensile tails. They live in elevated nests, move well through trees or cliffs, and are often feared as raiders, hunters, or mercenary fighters.

## Variants

- **Snow Garks:** Arctic-adapted Garks.
- **Jungle Garks:** Forest and rainforest Garks.
- **Cliff Garks:** High-place Garks.

## Physical Notes

Garks have thick ape-like bodies, long arms, strong tails, and dense grey fur. They are short but physically tough and well suited to climbing or elevated terrain.

## Mechanical Notes

- The source frames Garks as physically durable and useful as Fighters, scouts, spies, or Rogues.
- They may not become non-Channeling spell users.
- Their background options emphasize plunder, toughness, and mercenary usefulness.

## Languages

Starting Languages: Common-speech (S4/W4), plus one of Hill-speech (S8/W4), North-speech (S8/W4), or Wood-speech (S8/W4).

Allowed Adolescent Development: Common-speech (S8/W6), Hill-speech (S10/W8), North-speech (S10/W8), Wood-speech (S10/W8).

Shadow World starting languages: Gark (S8/W4), native regional language (S8/W4).

Shadow World allowed adolescent development: Gark (S10/W8), native regional language (S10/W8).

## Culture Notes

Gark societies are usually clan-scale and not highly complex. In Shadow World, some are trained by dark powers as military forces or mercenaries.

## Typical Cultures

- Snow Garks: [[Arctic Culture]] or Arctic Militaristic.
- Jungle Garks: Sylvan Barbarian.
- Cliff Garks: Aerial Barbarian.

## Character Hooks

- A Gark scout is detached from a mercenary company for special work.
- A young Gark leaves a warband to pursue treasure, status, or a rumor of stronger magic.
- A Gark raised outside the usual mercenary pattern tries to build a different reputation in Aethergate.

## Mana World Notes

Garks can serve as feared borderland raiders or hired irregulars in Mana World. Their prehensile tails and elevated homes make them useful around cliff roads, forest ruins, or suspended structures touched by unstable mana.
