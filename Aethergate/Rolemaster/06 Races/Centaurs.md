# Centaurs

## Summary

Centaurs are presented as a consolidated race with several body-plan variants. The book treats Horse, Lion, and Lizard Centaurs separately for physical traits, temperament, and common culture choices.

## Variants

- **Horse Centaur:** Philosophical, scholarly, and relatively detached from material wealth. Often best suited to mystical or healing roles.
- **Lion Centaur:** Proud, competitive, and socially hierarchical. Strong martial leanings and a natural claw attack.
- **Lizard Centaur:** Solitary and wandering by inclination, with coastal ties and some affinity for Sohleugir.

## Physical Notes

- **Horse Centaurs:** Human upper body with equine lower body; tall, long-lived, and physically imposing.
- **Lion Centaurs:** Human upper body with leonine lower body; fair-haired, prideful, and clawed.
- **Lizard Centaurs:** Human upper body with reptilian lower body; often mottled green, grey, black, or blue.

## Mechanical Notes

- Centaurs share Centaur-speech as a racial language.
- Lion Centaurs can make unarmed attacks as Medium Claw attacks.
- Centaurs may not choose the Extra Limbs talent in the same way as two-legged races.
- The source gives each Centaur variant five background options.

## Culture Notes

Centaurs are not presented as strongly religious or as having many unusual racial prejudices. Their social patterns vary sharply by variant: Horse Centaurs lean contemplative, Lion Centaurs competitive, and Lizard Centaurs independent.

## Typical Cultures

- Horse Centaur: [[Plains Culture]], with Highland, Rural, or Sylvan options for Shadow World analogues.
- Lion Centaur: [[Plains Culture]].
- Lizard Centaur: [[Littoral Culture]].

## Character Hooks

- A Horse Centaur healer or mystic answers a distant request for aid.
- A Lion Centaur fighter treats an expedition as a public test of honor.
- A Lizard Centaur monk or wanderer leaves the coast in search of rare knowledge.

## Mana World Notes

Centaurs could be tied to mana currents that pool in open country, especially where a Font cycle leaves long-lived geomantic tracks. Lizard Centaurs may suit Aethergate's coastal approaches or marshy trade edges, while Horse and Lion Centaurs fit frontier plains beyond the city's direct control.
