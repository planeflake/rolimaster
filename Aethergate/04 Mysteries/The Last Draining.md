# The Last Draining

## Overview

The Last Draining was the most recent known moment when the world was emptied of usable mana.

Later priests remember it as a war between light and darkness. Older fragments describe something colder: a divine war in which rival gods converted the world's mana into [[Channeling]] to feed their own power.

Millions died as cities, wards, crops, healing rites, engines, and spell traditions failed at once.

## Public Memory

Most surviving traditions reduce the event to a holy war.

In temple language, the powers of light spent themselves to hold back darkness. In darker cults, the war is remembered as the moment when shadow almost claimed the world.

Both versions hide the same wound: mortal civilization was used as fuel.

## Hidden Truth

The gods did not merely fight over the world.

They drank it.

As the war escalated, divine factions forced vast currents from [[The Nine Fonts]] through a catastrophic conversion into Channeling. This gave the gods direct, immediate power, but stripped the world below them of the mana needed for life, craft, healing, memory, and defense.

The conversion was unstable. Not all of the drained mana became Channeling cleanly. Some of it broke sideways into concentrated [[Essence]] and [[Mentalism]] storms, and those storms birthed new gods who were not part of the original war.

## New Gods

The Last Draining created at least two later divine lineages:

- Essence-born gods formed from raw pattern, force, light, shadow, material change, and destructive law.
- Mentalism-born gods formed from memory, fear, dream, command, prophecy, grief, and collective will.

These gods are younger than the old Channeling powers, but not necessarily weaker. They carry the trauma of their birth: they remember mortals not as worshippers, but as the shape left behind when mana was torn out of the world.

## Consequences

- Entire regions went silent for generations.
- Ancient wards failed at the same time, releasing things that had never been meant to walk free.
- Some civilizations preserved records only as dreams, scars, hymns, or impossible architecture.
- Later mana-conversion laws were shaped by fear of repeating the divine crime.
- The oldest taboos against Channeling conversion descend from this event.
- Some priests unknowingly serve gods born from the wreckage of the war they claim ended cleanly.

## Connection to the Lost Cycles

The Last Draining marks the boundary of at least one of [[The Lost Cycles]].

Dates before it are unreliable because the institutions that counted Cycles lost their mana-fed archives, oath chains, temple lights, and memory engines. Some records may not be missing; they may have been converted into divine power.

## Campaign Uses

- A modern mana shortage repeats a tiny version of the Last Draining.
- A Channeling surplus attracts attention from an old god who remembers the original conversion.
- A new Essence or Mentalism cult claims its god was born during the divine war, not after it.
- The party finds a pre-draining record that names the war as theft rather than sacrifice.
- A civic debate over [[Mana Conversion]] becomes dangerous when someone proves the practice has a divine precedent.
